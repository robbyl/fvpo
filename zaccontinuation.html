HIV and AIDS Situation

The National HIV prevalence in Zanzibar has remained stable at 0.6% since 2002 (ZACP 2002, THIMS 2008), with the Island of Unguja having a rate of 0.8% while the rate in Pemba Island, stands at 0.3% (THIMS 2008).

Although HIV prevalence is low in the general population, evidence shows that the rates are much higher among key population groups (Most-At-Risk Populations (MARPs)). Studies done on key population groups reveal that sex workers (CSW) have an HIV prevalence of 10.8%, Men who have Sex with Men (MSM) 12.3% (ZACP 2007), Injecting Drug Users (IDUs) 17% (ZACP 2006) and Prisoners 2.8% (ZACP 2008). The available evidence on the status of HIV in Zanzibar suggests that the island has a concentrated HIV epidemic. This means that the prevalence in general population is below one percent but is more than five percent in some groups within the society.

The drivers of the HIV epidemic in Zanzibar are linked to high risk behavior coupled with low perception of HIV transmission risk, stigma and discrimination, population mobility (for e.g. seasonal workers and fishermen), gender imbalances and other socio-economic and cultural factors such as high unemployment rates.

Institutional Perspectives (ZAC)

Vision of the Commission

To be strategic and efficient in coordinating the National multi- sectoral HIV and AIDS response in Zanzibar

Mission of the Department:

To lead, advocate, coordinate, monitor and evaluate the National Response against HIV and AIDS through ensuring availability of resources and guidelines, strengthened capacity of implementing partners and creating conducive environment for sustainable prevention of new infections, care and support for those infected and affected by the epidemic in Zanzibar.

Vision of Zanzibar National Strategic Plan I (ZNSP I) 2004/5-2009/10

To have Zanzibar population which is free from HIV AND AIDS threat and which has a sense of caring and supporting all those citizens infected and affect by HIV AND AIDS.

Mission of ZNSP I

To provide coordination leadership to a national multi-sectoral response to HIV AND AIDS leading to the reduction of HIV infections and its consequential socio-economic impact as well as to the reduction of unfavorable socio-economic factors that fuel the epidemic.

Overall objective of ZNSP I

Reduce new HIV infections by 50% by 2007 and provide treatment, care and support to people living with HIV and AIDS and their affected families.

Responsibilities

Zanzibar AIDS Commission (ZAC) has the mandate to coordinate HIV/AIDS National response in Zanzibar. The main responsibilities of ZAC among others are to:-

Ensure the development of strategies and policies in combating HIV and AIDS

Lob and advocate for HIV/AIDS issues

Enhance the capacity of the various sectors, HIV actors including community for effective participation on the HIV National Respond.

Mobilize and manage resources for HIV responses at all level

Encourage the provision of key basic needs and support to PLHAs

Monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of the strategies and policies for HIV response

Rationale for addressing HIV and AIDS in Poverty Reduction Programme

Both Vision 2020 and MKUZA recognize the existing link between HIV and AIDS and poverty. Level of Poverty is one among the factors fueling HIV transmission by increasing vulnerability and likelihood of exposure to infection especially among women and young people. On the other hand, HIV and AIDS erode the active labor force in all sectors if left unaddressed. The Vision 2020 as translated in the MKUZA recognize the implication of HIV and AIDS on economic and social development, in terms of inability of the labor force to effectively meet the demands of the society in the production sectors and social services; as well as reduction of family labor, and other demands in the household care and dependency (ZNSP 1, 2004/05 – 2008/09).

Vision 2020 Priorities

The following priorities in regards to HIV and AIDS are envisioned to be achieved by the year 2020:-

Establish reliable epidemic control measures

Well trained work force, equipped and financed

To raise the level of community awareness in both urban and rural areas

To provide special emphasis on the war against the spread of HIV through popular mass education programmes for behavior change
