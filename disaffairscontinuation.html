Department of Disability Affairs (DDA)

Successes of the DDA

DDA has the following successes:-

  • Advocate for equal rights and opportunity of people with disabilities (PWDs) to different stakeholders including lawyers, local leaders, media and community at large.

  • Provided consultancy to public and private institutions on accessibility and friendly environment for PWDs including Zanzibar Social Security Fund (ZSSF), Zanzibar Revenue Board (ZRB), House of Representatives, Municipal Council and others.

  • Established Zanzibar Development Fund for persons with disabilities.

  • Developed Zanzibar National Disability Council Action Plan,

  • Presented disability issues on UN coordination meeting for the purpose of introducing DDA /Disability National Council held in UN Office in Zanzibar.

  • Supported educational and medical support to PWDs (Annex )

  • Provided assistive device to PWDs including 95 wheelchairs

  • Built capacity building of staff and Council members on disability issues. These include 4 trainings for 13 council members, three days training on Inclusive education for 35 staff from First Vice Presidents Office/DDA, one year secretariat course for 1 staff, three days trainings on project write -up for 7 staff of the DDA.

  • Organized and coordinated 12 regular and 10 extra ordinary meetings of the Council as well as civic and voters education to persons with disabilities and DPOs.

  • Strengthened networking and collaboration with National and International Organizations dealing with disability issues.

  • Created awareness and sensitized on the mainstreaming of PWDs issues to 10 District Commissioners and 10 Administrators in Unguja and Pemba.

  • Prepared project proposals on improving accessibility to built environment, Community Based Rehabilitation (CBR) and research paper on improving social services to PWDs.

  • Participated in National and International meetings, conferences and workshops related to disability issues. This include; UN meetings, EAC and disability regional meetings.

 

  • Organized and conducted 2 visits by council members and staff of the DDA to Disabled Peoples Organizations (DPO’s) in Unguja and Pemba.

  • Conducted one study tour for DDA staff to institutions dealing with disability issues in Tanzania Mainland. Institutions visited including Tanzania Social welfare Department, Salvation Army and others.

  • Conducted need assessments for youth with disabilities in collaboration with Youth Challenge International (YCI)

  • Commemorate UN International Day for PWDs each year in Unguja and Pemba.

  • Built capacity of DDA by purchasing office equipments and recruit human resources

  • Financial support to DPO’s

  • Giving PWD’s charity through other source like donors

  • Sending youth with disabilities in short courses

Challenges of the DDA

The key challenges identified in the implementation of DDA objectives are as follows:-

  • Lack of adequate data on disability (no reliable and accurate data on disability issues and research done, documented/published)

  • Inadequate human and financial resources

  • Lack of expertise in the Disability issues

  • Insufficiency of transport facilities and office equipments

  • Inaccessible environment (poor infrastructure/ building, transport and so on)

  • Increased demand of PWD needs in terms of medical and educational support.

  • Low level of awareness on disability policy and legislations at the community.

  • Low acceptance of stakeholders in supporting PWDs

  • Zanzibar Disability Policy and Law need to be reflected to UN Convention on the Rights of People with Disabilities (UNCRPD) and other International Instruments.

 

Way forward

DDA intends to continue with a sustained commitment to disability activities across all program areas and looking forward to working closely with different stakeholders to ensure the successful delivery of its objectives. The following areas need to be considered as follows:-

  • There is need for government to provide sufficient funds in implementing disability issues

  • Self- representation of PWDs in disability issues and decision making.

  • Capacity building and awareness raising on PWDs to the community.

  • Establish disability focal point in the Ministries

  • Review Zanzibar Disability Policy and Law.

  • Develop DDA Strategic and Operational plan

  • Provisions of appliances/assistive devices such as hearing aids, white canes, crutches ,sunscreen lotions and others

  • Awareness creation on disability issues in the society is vital in order to change people mindset.
