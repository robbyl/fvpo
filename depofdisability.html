Department of Disability Affairs (DDA)

The Revolutionary Government of Zanzibar recognizes the importance of persons with Disabilities in its effort to reduce poverty and making the right to development a reality for everyone including persons with disabilities (PWDs). It should be noted that, people with disabilities are among the most vulnerable groups in the society. They are often undereducated, unemployed or underemployed especially women and youth with disabilities. This is a special vulnerable group that needs social protection.

Service to persons with disabilities in Zanzibar was introduced by Disabled Peoples Organizations (DPOs) in the1980s. In 2008, The Revolutionary Government of Zanzibar established Zanzibar National Council for persons with disabilities (ZNCPWDs) and the Department of Disability Affairs (DDA) through Zanzibar Disability Act No 9 of 2006 (Rights and Privileges) with the purpose of coordinating, facilitating, educating, advocating on equalization of opportunities and rights for PWDs in Zanzibar. This Act recognizes the equal rights and forbids discrimination in employment, denial access to health, education and other services. Furthermore, the Act encourages access to barrier – free environment, exemption from taxation on some disability aids and equal rights before the courts.

The Department acts as a secretariat of Zanzibar National Disability Council for Persons with Disabilities, also guided by the Zanzibar Disability Policy (2004) , Disability Act (2006) and UN Convention on the Rights of People with Disabilities (UNCRPD) which United Republic of Tanzania (URT) signed in March, 2007 and ratified in November, 2009.

Orgainzational structure of the DDA

Vision

To enhance quality life of people with disability by strengthening institutional capacity of Zanzibar National Council for Persons with Disabilities (ZNCPWDs) through promoting management practices of the DDA and mainstreaming disability issues in all aspect of development.

Mission

“To be efficient and effective in providing quality services and times response to people with disability needs”

This will be pursed through the following four major themes.

  • Supporting people with disabilities community wellbeing.

  • Facilitating of employment opportunity to PWDs.

  • Integrating of various aspects for the benefit of the PWDs community.

  • Sustainable development for the community of PWDs.

Core functions

As stipulated in the Disability Act, 2006 the functions of the DDA are as follows:-

    1. To co-ordinate activities of persons with disabilities and others stakeholders;
    2. To formulate strategies, measures and program for the protection and promotion of human rights for person with Disabilities in order to achieve equal opportunities for people with disabilities in education, training, employment and others areas.
    3. To co-ordinate programmes for the protection and promotion of human rights for persons with disabilities.
    4. To co-ordinate registration of person with disabilities, their institutions, and organization.
    5. To give effect to any International treaty relating to the welfare or rehabilitation of persons with disabilities. 
    6. To perform any function related to the protection, improvement of the social and economic status of persons with disabilities;
    7. To perform any function assigned by the Council.

 

Key functions of the DDA units

Monitoring and Evaluation Unit

  • Monitoring different activities of the DDA.
  • Making evaluation of the activities that have been done by the DDA

Mobilization, Media and Communication Unit

  • Sensitize the society on different issues about people with disability
  • Advocate the rights and privileges of people with disabilities in Zanzibar.
  • Production of different publications on issues related with people with disabilities.

Planning, Policy, Research and Projects Unit

  • Planning the programmes that will benefit the current and future of people with disabilities.
  • Planning annual expenditure of the DDA

Administration and Financial Management Unit

  • Administer human resource in the DDA.
  • Administer funds from the government and donors.

Guidelines of the DDA

The DDA has three main guidelines, namely:-

    1. Development Policy of the People with Disability (2004)
    2. Disability Act (Rights and Privileges) Act No. 9 of 2006
    3. UN Convention of the People with Disabilities (UNCPWD’s).

Key partners of the DDA

DDA is working with close relationship with Disability Persons Organizations (DPO’s), those DPO’s are:-

    1. Organization of People with Disabilities (UWZ).
    2. Zanzibar Association of the Deaf (CHAVIZA)
    3. Special Olympic Zanzibar (SOZ)
    4. Zanzibar Association of the Blind (ZANAB)
    5. Sports Association of Disabilities in Zanzibar (SAD’Z)
    6. Zanzibar Center of Disability and Inclusive Development (ZACDID)
    7. Zanzibar Association of People with Developmental Disabilities (ZAPDD)
    8. Zanzibar Association of Albino (JMZ)
    9. Zanzibar Association of Women with Disabilities (JUWAUZA)
