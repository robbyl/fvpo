Institutional Prospectives (ZAC)

Vision of the Commission

To be strategic and efficient in coordinating the National multi- sectoral HIV and AIDS response in Zanzibar

Mission of the Commission:

To lead, advocate, coordinate, monitor and evaluate the National Response against HIV and AIDS through ensuring availability of resources and guidelines, strengthened capacity of implementing partners and creating conducive environment for sustainable prevention of new infections, care and support for those infected and affected by the epidemic in Zanzibar.

Vision of Zanzibar National Strategic Plan I (ZNSP I) 2004/5-2009/10

To have Zanzibar population which is free from HIV AND AIDS threat and which has a sense of caring and supporting all those citizens infected and affect by HIV AND AIDS.

Mission of ZNSP I

To provide coordination leadership to a national multi-sectoral response to HIV AND AIDS leading to the reduction of HIV infections and its consequential socio-economic impact as well as to the reduction of unfavorable socio-economic factors that fuel the epidemic.

Zanzibar AIDS Commission (ZAC)

Zanzibar AIDS Commission is a legal corporate body that was established by the Act No. 3 of 2003 of the Revolutionary Government of Zanzibar by the House of Representatives.

It is an independent department within the Office of the First Vice President from the year 2010. The overall objective of the Commission is to coordinate the national HIV and AIDS response in Zanzibar. The Commission has the following core functions:-

•  To ensure the development of strategies and policies in combating HIV and AIDS;

•  To control and ameliorate the effects of the HIV and AIDS epidemic and to promote and co-ordinate the application of such strategies and policies;

•  To mobilize and manage resources, whether financial or otherwise in support to the National response to HIV and AIDS;

•  To enhance the capacity of the various sectors of the community to respond to the HIV and AIDS epidemic and to co-ordinate their responses;

•  To encourage the provision of facilities to treat and care for persons infected with HIV and AIDS and their dependents;

•  To monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of the strategies and policies referred to in paragraph (1) and generally, the national response to HIV and AIDS;

•  To disseminate and encourage the dissemination of information on all aspects of HIV and AIDS;

To exercise any other function that may be conferred on the Commission by the Act or any other enactment

In fulfilling its roles and responsibilities, ZAC is working within the multi - sectoral framework; leaving every sector to play its part within the mandates, role and responsibilities and comparative advantages. ZAC works with stakeholders from the following constituencies: public sectors, Civil Society Organizations (CSOs) including faith based institutions and communities, private sectors, higher learning institutions and informal sector.

Rationale for addressing HIV and AIDS in Poverty Reduction Programme

Both Vision 2020 and MKUZA recognize the existing link between HIV and AIDS and poverty. Level of Poverty is one among the factors fueling HIV transmission by increasing vulnerability and likelihood of exposure to infection especially among women and young people.

On the other hand, HIV and AIDS erode the active labor force in all sectors if left unaddressed. The Vision 2020 as translated in the MKUZA recognize the implication of HIV and AIDS on economic and social development, in terms of inability of the labor force to effectively meet the demands of the society in the production sectors and social services; as well as reduction of family labor, and other demands in the household care and dependency (ZNSP 1, 2004/05 â€“ 2008/09).
