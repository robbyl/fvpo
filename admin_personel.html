    Establishment of internal audit:

    The internal audit services will be governed by the following laws, roles ,regulations and procedures :

    • Public finance act no.12(2005)and its regulations.
    • Public procurement act no.9(2005) and its regulations.
    • Employment act no. 11(2005) and its regulations.
    • Finance circular (given by Accountant general regularly)
    • Public service circulars .
    • Institute of internal auditors principles and standards.
    • Internal accounting standards.
    • Government orders.
    • Audit manual
    • MTEF and Ministerial budget.

    Authority.

    Authority is granted for full, free and unrestricted access to any all of Ministerial records, physical properties ,and personnel relevant to any function under review .All employees are requested to assist internal auditing in fulfilling their staff function. Internal auditor shall also have free and unrestricted access to the accounting officer, and the audit committee .

    Documents and information given to Internal auditing during a periodic review will be handled in the same prudent and confidentially manner as by those employees normally accountable for them.

    Responsibility.

    The duties and responsibilities of internal audit functions are as stipulated in the regulation 34(1)(a-b)public finance regulations. These include reviewing and reporting on,

    • Proper control over receipts and payments.
    • Conformity with the financial and operation procedures and good accounting practices.
    • Correct classification and allocation of revenue and expenditures account.
    • Reliability and integrity of financial and operating data,so as to ensure preparation of accurate financial statements and other reports as per legislatives requirements.
    • system in place used to safeguards assets.
    • Programs/Operation to ensure consistence with established objectives and goals.
    • Adequacy of actions by management in response to recommendations made on the internal audit reports and those from the controller and Auditor general

    Accountability.

    The internal audit unit shall be accountable to:

    • The management (Accounting Officer)
    • The audit committee to the following ,
    • Provide annual assessment on the adequacy and effectiveness of the internal controls.
    • Coordinate with and provide the oversight of other controls and monitoring functions (eg Risk management, security, ethics and environment)
    • Provide information on the status of audit annual audit plan and sufficiency of resources of departments.
    • Reporting on the issue related to the processes for controlling the activities of the Ministry.
