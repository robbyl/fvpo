Department of Environment (DoE)

Institutional Perspectives(DoE)

The Department of Environment was established in 1989 under the Chief Minister's Office afterward it has been transferred to the Commission for Land and Environment from 1990 to 2002. From the year 2002 to 2010 the Department was under the Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Environment. Currently, it is under the Office of the First Vice President of Zanzibar. This Department has a key role in achieving the national goal of sustainable development set out in the National Development Vision 2020.

 

Our vision

  • To attain Sustainable and Sound Environment Management in Zanzibar

Our mission

  • To address, manage, ragulate and enforce all enviromental issues, objectives and processes affecting the environmental resource base of islands in a sustainable, integrated and participatory approach

  • To promote protection and conservation of the environment through environmental awareness, natural resources management, mainstreaming of environmental management principle in the development project, assessing and monitoring the quality of the environment, enforcing the environmental policy, legislation, regulation, standards and guidelines

Our guiding principles

  • The Right to Life and Healthy Environment for all.

  • Principles of Sustainable Development and Inter-Generational Equity

  • Precautionary Principle, Scientific Information and Social Responsibility

  • Resource Efficiency, Green Technologies, and Cleaner Production.

  • Environmental Education, Awareness, and Accountability.

  • Climate Change Adaptation and Mitigation.

Our objectives

  • To strengthen environmental management, protection and conservation of terrestrial and off-shore natural resources of Zanzibar.

  • To enhance partnership between sectorial institutions and non state actors in sustainable management of our environment.

  • To promote resource efficiency and cleaner production in the context of an integrated coastal zone management framework and protocols

  • To Integrate environmental concerns into the Zanzibar development policies, plans, programs and projects.

  • To promote policy dialogue, awareness, and environmental ethics at all levels of the society in the efforts to raise public environmental responsibility.

  • To address environmental concerns of Zanzibar at the regional and international forums in line with the environment-related conventions and protocols.

Our mandate

  • Enforce environmental Management for sustainable Development Act, 1996, Zanzibar, and all its subsequent Regulations.

  • Review, assess, and improve, and update environmental regulations, policies, guidelines, programs and strategic environmental assessments in conjunction with the goals of the Zanzibar Development Vision 2020 and Zanzibar Poverty Reduction Strategy (MKUZA-II)

  • Coordinate with the Office of the President of Zanzibar on all international environmental obligations and the position of Zanzibar towards regional international environmental conventions and protocols.

  • Coordinate as the national secretariat in the implementation of Climate Change adaptation and mitigation actions

  • Prevent all types of domestic and industrial pollution and enforce the prescription of environmental control standards on atmospheric, terrestrial, aquatic, marine and noise pollution.

  • Promote integrated solid waste management practices and mobilize prevention of indiscriminate waste disposal and control import of hazardous substances.

  • Conduct environmental Screening, Scoping, and Reviewing of all development projects falling within the Schedules of the Environmental Management Act requiring Environmental Impact Assessment

  • Register and Approve qualified Environmental Impact Assessment Consultants and Professionals.

  • Conduct Enhance Environmental Education and Public Awareness by training, publications, and media dissemination.

  • Carry out, coordinate, advice, and facilitate scientific research and survey on environmental status, threats, management, protection, and conservation.

Institutional structure of the department

The Department of Environment comprises of five specialized sections, with the Head of each Section reporting to the Director who runs the Administration Unit of the Department. These Sections are:

  • Environmental Education Section,

  • Environmental Impact Assessment Section,

  • Natural Resources Management Section

  • Pollution Prevention and Control Section

  • Policy Planning & Research and Section

The Pemba Head Office of the Department of Environment is headed by the Officer-in-Charge who reports to the Director and supervises the functions of the respective sections for Pemba Island.

The District responsibilities are covered by District Environmental Officers in all 10 Districts of Zanzibar. The Districts Environmental Officers are responsible for overseeing all Departmental activities in their respective Districts.

Our current institutional targets

  • New Zanzibar Environment Policy unveiled and operational by 2011/2012.

  • Zanzibar Climate Change Steering and Technical Committees established by 2011/2012.

  • Zanzibar Climate Change Strategy developed, accepted, and operational by 2012.

  • Advocacy and Awareness Programs on REDD+ for Zanzibar active by 2011/2012.

  • Implementation of the Mauritius Strategy on Sustainable Development for Small Island Developing States functional by 2011/2012.

  • Environmental Monitoring and Evaluation Framework reviewed, developed and improved by 2011/2012.

  • The Sustainable Utilization of Non-Renewable Natural Resources Regulations, adopted and operational by the first quarter of 2012.

  • The Integrated Coastal Zone Management Regulations developed and assented by 2012.

  • Integrated Coastal Zone Management Strategy and Costing for planned activities ready an operational by 2011/2012

  • Waste Electrical and Electronics Equipment (Management and Handling) Regulations functional by 2012.

  • New environmental standards for potable water, atmospheric and waste water effluents developed, adopted and operational by 2012.

  • Schedules of the Act that allow for prosecution of offenders of Plastic Carry Bags Regulations are ready and operable by 2011/2012.

  • Enhanced media dissemination, awareness, publications, and training, fully operational by 2011/2012

  • Indian Crow eradication programme fully operational by 2011/2012.

Our major international partners and projects

  • Marine and Coastal and Environment Management Project (MACEMP) supported by the World Bank

  • Sustainable Management for Lands and Environment – II Project supported by the Government of Finland.

  • Africa Adaptation Program of the United Nations Development Program supported by the Government of Japan.

  • Millennium Challenge Account – Tanzania Project supported by the United States of America.

  • Regional Coastal Management Project (ReCoMAP) of the Indian Ocean Commission supported by the European Union

New donor funded projects

  • Environmental and Climate Change (Zanzibar Component) of the United Nation as Development Assistance Program (UNDAP)

  • Mauritius Strategy of Implementation for Small Island Developing States supported by the Indian Ocean Commission.

  • The UK Department for International Development (DFID) – funded project called " Economic Impacts of Climate Change in Zanzibar "
