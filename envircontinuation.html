Department of Environment (DoE)

Administration unit

The Administration Unit is the lead office headed by the Director and which establishes the vital link between the Department’s operations and communications with the rest of the world. The major objective of the unit is the monitoring and evaluation of the set institutional targets while conforming to the vision, mission, guiding principles, and the overall objectives of the Department. The Unit consists of the following areas:

  • Finance operations
  • Procurement services
  • Legal compliance
  • Public Administration
  • Correspondences
  • Store-Keeping and
  • Chauffeurs

Environmental education section

Core responsibilities of this section include

  • Public awareness on environment and biodiversity conservation.
  • Planning and implementation of environmental communications and outreach programs using media dissemination, public lectures, and publications.
  • Environmental advocacy and promotional programs for public interest against environmental degradation and pollution.
  • Develop training needs assessment and capacity building for the institution.

Currently, the Section is specifically involved in:

  • The development of Zanzibar’s first ever Environmental Information Management System (ZEIMS) and Electronic Databank Repository
  • Planning and development of environmental monitoring and evaluation framework.
  • Preparation of the strategic planning on environment.

Environmental Impact Assessment and Auditing Section (EIA)

The section is primarily responsible for environmental clearance of all industrial and development projects including those falling within the Schedules of the Environmental Management for Sustainable Development Act, Zanzibar, 1996.

The main objective of this Section is to ensure that environmental safeguards are integrated in all sectors of the economy from the feasibility stage, planning, designing, construction, operational, and finally in the decommissioning phases of any proposed project.

Major functions of the EIA Section include:

  • Registration of all industrial and development projects requiring environmental clearance.
  • Registration and Approval of all EIA Consultants.
  • Carrying out Screening and Scoping procedures of proposed projects.
  • Developing Terms of Reference (ToR) for carrying out Environmental Impact Assessment study.
  • Reviewing Environmental Impact Statement of a proposed project.
  • Receiving Stakeholder and Public Comments on the Environmental Impact Statement of a proposed project.
  • Conducting Stakeholder Review Analysis on the Environmental Impact Statement of a proposed project.
  • Recommending for approval or rejection of an Environmental Impact Statement.
  • Carrying out Environmental and Social Monitoring Plan of a project through site inspections/verification visits.
  • Conducting regular environmental monitoring
  • Commissioning external environmental audit of a project to verify compliance or conformity.
  • Managing an environmental clearance databank of all industrial and development projects in Zanzibar.

Pollution Prevention and Control Section

The Section is primarily responsible for environmental enforcement of the quality of air, water, and land. Major tasks of the Section include:

  • Developing regulations, standards and guidelines on waste minimization and pollution control.
  • Monitoring ambient air quality from domestic and industrial emissions.
  • Monitoring water and waste water quality from the sources to the supply systems.
  • Enforcing plastic carry bags ban through surprise visits and site inspections.
  • Supervising handling and appropriate disposal of hazardous substances.
  • Enforcing appropriate actions against marine dumping and pollution.
  • Collaborating with principal stakeholders on public health and sanitation.
  • Developing and implementing policies and strategies to avoid and minimize waste.
  • Prescribing advisory warning on environmental and health status of public beaches places, products, and processes

The Section is currently working on the draft regulations to control the import of Waste Electrical and Electronics Equipment (E-Waste) that will protect the fragile island ecosystems from dangerous and toxic substances associated with importation of E-Waste materials.

The Section is still spearheading enforcement of plastic carry bags ban in Zanzibar.

Natural Resources Management Section

The major tasks of this Section include:

  • Protection, conservation, and sustainable management of terrestrial, aquatic and marine ecosystems.
  • Development of standards, regulations, and guidelines on Integrated Coastal Zone Management (ICZM)
  • Implementation of the Integrated Coastal Zone Management Strategy and Costing activities
  • Implementation of the Integrated Coastal Zone Management Planning activities.
  • Collaboration and networking on protection of the islands from Avian flu
  • Advocacy programs in the implementation of REDD project in Zanzibar.
  • Support to the development of the REDD Strategy for the United Republic of Tanzania
  • Implementation of the Africa Adaptation Program on Climate Change adaptation.
  • Facilitating the development of the Climate Change Strategy for Zanzibar.
  • Enforcement of regulatory powers on sustainable utilization of non-renewable natural resources.
  • Implementation of the programs and plans related to Protected Areas Board and Biosphere Reserves.

Policy Planning & Research Section

This Section is responsible for environmental planning and research in various areas, ranging from terrestrial, aquatic, coastal, and marine ecosystems, climate change, pollution prevention, environmental impact assessment, and in environmental education. Major tasks include:

  • Providing technical solutions that can help in decision-making process by the Government.
  • Review and Assessment of Environmental policies, guidelines, standards and regulations.
  • Developing Plan, Strategies, and Action Plans for the implementation of various Department’s programs and projects e.g. Climate Change, Mauritius Strategy, Waste Management, etc.
  • Re-assess compliance and status of enforcement of environmental regulations
  • Support the implementation of the Strategic Environmental Planning.
  • Support, facilitate and carry out various environmental research projects, programs, and surveys.
