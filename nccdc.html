Commission's Objectives and Core Functions

Objectives

•  To build conducive environment for the implementation of instructional and legal framework.

•  To eliminate the supply and availability of illicit drug for use in or for trafficking in Zanzibar.

•  To enhance collaboration and coordination among regional and international countries in all matters related to drug abuse.

•  To prevent the community on drug abuse and other related problems.

•  To provide counseling, treatment, rehabilitation and after care services.

Core functions

•  To coordinate substance abuse affairs for collaboration with other stakeholders National and International.

•  To advocate the society on effect of drug abuse through different channels such as mass media, education institution, grass root level meeting (shehia) and in working place.

•  Counseling and treatment for the addicts and their families

The National Commission for Coordination and Drugs Control (NCCDC)

This Commission was established in April 1996 under the Chief Minister's office (CMO). In 2000 this commisson was moved to the Ministry of Health and Social Welfare. Currently DSA is under the set up of the First Vice President Office (FVPO).

Current situation

Zanzibar is located at strategic corridor for drug trafficking. For the past two decades it has been observing an incremental growth of the challenging exponential use of illegal substance among the youth and young Zanzibaris. It is estimated that 3.1% of the adult drug using population are injecting drug users. Both males and females have been implicated in drug trafficking activities.

Substance abuse is a worldwide public health problem. There are number of problems of public concerns regarding young people who misuse drugs. In addition, Substance abuse causes a significant burden to individuals and societies. In Zanzibar, substance abuse and specifically, injecting drug use is among the factors that lead to increase in HIV infections among IDUs. Other infections caused are Hepatitis B and C and syphilis. The prevalence of HIV among the IDUs is 26.8% which is higher than the prevalence rate of 0.6% of the general population.

Substance abuse has negative effects on the labour and productive forces due to the fact that most users are young people. Vision 2020 and MKUZA1 recognize the need to support the community especially youth to prevent from drug abuse.

Vision 2020 and MKUZA priorities

•  Develop and implement effective policies, strategies and activities to eliminate gender based violence.

•  Increase awareness of HIV/AIDS in related risks especially in young people.

•  Strengthen HIV/AIDS prevention education, with special focus on young and MARPs.

•  Establishment of detoxification centers for substance abusers in both Islands.
