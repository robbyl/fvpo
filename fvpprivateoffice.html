First Vice President Private Office

Overview

The Private Office was established in November 2010 soon after sworn in the First Vice President following General Election which was held in 2010. The election result together with the constitutional amendments in Zanzibarhas lead to the formation of Government of National Unity (GNU). According to the constitution,the First Vice President position was put on place in order to improve democratic institution and national unity. The First Vice President office has a mandate to coordinates all activities of the First Vice President of Zanzibar.

Location of the office

Private Office is located at the main building of the First Vice President Office, second floor at Migombani Zanzibar.

Overall objective

  • To coordinate, monitor and supervise allThe First Vice President activities.

Targets to overcome the overall objective

  • To coordinates the First Vice President’s activities.

  • To create a vibrant Information and communication section.

  • To ensure the availability of necessary materials and equipments at the first vice president’s resident and office

  • Continuously improve the human resource at work place.

  • To facilitate consultation and feedback mechanisms between elected leaders and community members.

Organisation structure

The Private Office so far comprises with 27staffs (15 male and 12 female) divided into two places that arethe office wing andhome wing with different cadres.

Private Secretary and his Deputy Secretary generally administer all activities and staffs located in both sides.

(i) Monitoring and evaluation unit (M&E)

This section is preliminary responsible for gathering of information and its analysis in order to determine whether progress is being made towards pre-specified goals, objectives and highlights whether there are any externalities from a project or its activities.

Function of M & E Units:

  • Provision of information needed to coordinate the human, financial and physical resources committed to the project or program to improve performance.

  • Provision of information to inform setting and adjustment of objectives and strategies

  • Provide regular feedback on project performance and show any need for mid course corrections.

  • Identify challenges at an early stage and propose means to solve it.

  • Monitor project services and outcome by the targets population.

  • Incorporate stakeholders’ views and promote participation, ownership and accountability.

  • Prepare government implementation reports monthly, quarterly and annually or whenever needed.
