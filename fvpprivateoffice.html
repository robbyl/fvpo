First Vice President Private Office

Overview

The Private Office was established in November 2010 soon after sworn in the First Vice President following General Election which was held in 2010. The election result together with the constitutional amendments in Zanzibarhas lead to the formation of Government of National Unity (GNU). According to the constitution,the First Vice President position was put on place in order to improve democratic institution and national unity. The First Vice President office has a mandate to coordinates all activities of the First Vice President of Zanzibar.

Overall objectives

  • To coordinate plans, policies and research that fall under FVPO

  • To supervise the development of the projects

  • To coordinate and supervise the budget preparation.

  • To prepare the quarterly and annual implementation reports

  • To undertake supervision, monitoring and evaluation of the FVPO projects and programs

  • Coordinate researches carried out in the FVPO’s institutions

Objectives

  • To identify, coordinate and supervise research programs for evidence based decision and planning

  • To coordinate preparation of policies, strategies, plans and coordinate all development projects under the First Vice President’s Office

  • To make follow-ups, and carry out data analysis for evaluation and reporting on short term and long term basis

  • To build capacity of staff for effective and efficiency service delivery

  • To coordinate mainstreaming of cross cutting issues (HIV/ AIDS, Gender, Disability, Environment, Drugs, Population and Good Governance) in FVPO programs and activities.

  • To coordinate and mobilize resource to facilitate implementation of FVPO’s core functions.

Institutional structure of the department

The Department of Planning, Policy and Research consists of four specialized sections, with the head of each section reporting to the Director.

These sections are

  • Monitoring and Evaluation Unit

  • Policy development Unit

  • Research Unit.

  • Sectoral Planning and Development Unit.

Planning Officers from all Department under FVPO work directly with Department of Planning, Policy and Research.

Director of Planning, Policy and Research

The Director of Planning, Policy and Research is the overall in charge and head of the department with major roles of supervision and control of departments in the FVPO. The Director also has the following responsibilities among others-:

  • To ensure better utilization of resources.

  • To supervise, review and assess policy, plans, programs and projects.

  • To coordinate research activities and policy preparation

  • To supervise units and control the departmental responsibilities.

  • To ensure all departmental needs are prepared and controlled in a right time according to the government guidelines and stakeholders needs.

(i) Monitoring and evaluation unit (M&E)

This section is preliminary responsible for gathering of information and its analysis in order to determine whether progress is being made towards pre-specified goals, objectives and highlights whether there are any externalities from a project or its activities.

Function of M & E Units:

  • Provision of information needed to coordinate the human, financial and physical resources committed to the project or program to improve performance.

  • Provision of information to inform setting and adjustment of objectives and strategies

  • Provide regular feedback on project performance and show any need for mid course corrections.

  • Identify challenges at an early stage and propose means to solve it.

  • Monitor project services and outcome by the targets population.

  • Incorporate stakeholders’ views and promote participation, ownership and accountability.

  • Prepare government implementation reports monthly, quarterly and annually or whenever needed.

(ii) Policy Development Unit:

Function of Policy Development Unit:

  • Coordinate and Provide guidance on preparation and review of policies

  • Analyze existing policies and create conducive implementation modalities.

  • Undertake deep analysis of economic, social, administrative issues and advise FVPO to prepare policies based on demand.

  • Work closely with the Planning Commission during implementation and development of new policies and ensure its alignment with existing national strategies including MKUZA II and VISION 2020.

(iii) Research unit

Functions of Research unit:

  • Conducting and analyzing researches.

  • Conduct FVPO service delivery assessment.

  • Work closely with high learning institutions in research and analysis in areas of interest and relevance to the FVPO.

(iv) Sectoral planning and development unit:

This section is responsible for day to day planning activities including preparation of budgets, strategic plans and implementation plan as well as coordination of projects and programs implementation.

Function of Sectoral Planning and Development unit:

  • Preparation of annual operational plan and Midterm Strategic Plan.

  • Work closely with Planning Commission during preparation of new plans in order to make sure its alignment with implementation of national strategies including MKUZA II and VISION 2020 and sectoral development strategies.

  • Coordinate the preparation of FVPO Budget.

  • Coordinate projects and program implementation report.

  • Coordinate FVPO private partnerships

  • Analyze and interpret statistical information related to economic and social development

  • Collect statistical information to support in setting priorities for social and community development
